ALGAR
TELECOM

The app had a 1.9-star rating. Users couldn't pay their bills, so they flooded the call center. We had to stop the bleeding before making it beautiful.

The initial ask was a visual revamp. I pushed back. We pivoted the roadmap to focus exclusively on stabilizing the core transactional flows: Login, Billing, and Support.

Role UX Designer
Client Algar Telecom
Duration 1 Year
Focus Critical Flows & Stability
01 //

The Crisis

What was broken & why it mattered

Millions of users relied on the app, but broken payment and login flows turned simple tasks into multi-minute frustrations. 80% of support tickets came from these two journeys. The app wasn't just failing users; it was costing the business millions in call center operations.

Instability

Excessive crashes and latency turned simple transactions into multi-minute frustrations.

Broken Flows

Payment and login bugs blocked the two most critical user journeys — generating 80% of tickets.

Confusing Navigation

Non-intuitive architecture, insufficient error feedback, high abandonment.

Dead-End Support

In-app support channels were inoperative → users overflowed to call center (most expensive path).

Artifact: Journey Map / Support Ticket Analysis

Show a visual breakdown of where users were dropping off or a chart showing the spike in support tickets tied to specific app flows. This proves you didn't just guess the problem; you found it in the data.

↗ Evidence: Systems Thinking

Mapped the full end-to-end journey to find where a "small" self-service change would break downstream support escalation paths — preventing a cascade of edge-case failures.

02 //

The Pivot

Choosing stability over aesthetics

The initial brief was a visual redesign to make the app look modern. But a pretty app that crashes is still a broken app. I pushed back and advocated for a complete pivot: freeze all new features and focus entirely on fixing the core transactional flows.

Artifact: Roadmap Pivot / Stakeholder Presentation Slide

Show a before/after of the product roadmap, or a slide you used to convince stakeholders to stop the visual redesign and focus on stability. This proves your strategic influence and ability to push back.

01. The Trade-off

We cut a planned full billing redesign to ship critical payment fixes in 6 weeks. We removed heavy marketing banners to lighten the API load.

Result

Protected the activation funnel that drove 80% of support tickets.

02. The Alignment

Stakeholder priorities conflicted across domains. I used crash-report data and videos of users failing simple tasks to build empathy.

Result

Shifted the culture from "ship fast" to "ship validated."

03 //

The Execution

Designing around legacy tech

Managing Expectations

We couldn't fix the legacy backend overnight, so we designed around it. We introduced clear loading states and proactive error handling. We learned that users accepted longer processes if given real progress feedback.

Validation as a Rule

No solution shipped without validation. Moderated remote usability testing sessions became a required step in development (Definition of Ready).

Artifact: Error State Handoff / Usability Test Findings

Show a snippet of your handoff documentation detailing error states, or a summary of usability test findings that led to a specific design change. This proves your attention to detail and commitment to validation.

↗ Evidence: Technical Influence

Worked directly with engineering leads to identify which visual improvements would cause the least backend stress. Detailed error-behavior handoff documentation reduced rework and raised implementation quality.

04 //

The Aftermath

Restoring trust & reducing costs

By fixing the foundation, we restored trust. Users could finally self-serve without errors, and the business saw an immediate drop in operational costs. The app transformed from a frustration generator into a reliable utility.

Artifact: Final UI vs Legacy UI / Impact Dashboard

Show a side-by-side comparison of the old, broken flow vs the new, stable flow, or a dashboard showing the drop in support tickets. This proves the tangible business impact of your work.
